Part 0

The decision that gates everything

The EU AI Act is risk-tiered: what you must do depends entirely on which tier your system falls in. A prohibited system cannot ship at all. A high-risk one carries a heavy compliance programme. A limited-risk one needs only disclosure. A minimal-risk one needs nothing. So the first, gating task for any AI system is classification - and it is a decision you can learn to make quickly and defend. This session builds that reflex with a checker you drive.

Read this first Educational, not legal advice. The checker is a teaching model of the risk-tier logic - real classification depends on specific facts. Read Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 and get advice.

Part 1 · covers the decision logic

First match wins 7 min live

Classification runs in order: check prohibited first, then high-risk, then limited, else minimal - and assess GPAI as a parallel layer. The first tier that matches is the answer.

LiveThe order, and why order matters3 min▶
  • In scope? (Art 2) - if not (no EU market, no EU output), the Act does not apply. Everything below assumes you are in.
  • Prohibited? (Art 5) - the eight banned practices. If it matches, stop: it cannot ship, and the fine tier is the highest (7% / EUR 35M).
  • High-risk? (Art 6 + Annex III) - the eight use-case areas (employment, credit, education, biometrics, law enforcement...). This is where most consequential business AI lands.
  • Limited-risk? (Art 50) - chatbots, deepfakes, synthetic content. Disclosure and labelling only.
  • Minimal - everything else. No mandatory obligations.
  • GPAI (parallel): if the artefact is a general-purpose model, it carries its own obligations regardless of the tier of systems built on it.

Part 2 · the two nuances

Downgrade, and the profiling trap 8 min live

Two rules decide the close calls in the high-risk tier. One can pull a system down out of high-risk; the other slams it back in. Miss them and you will misclassify.

LiveThe Article 6(3) downgrade3 min▶

Not every system in an Annex III area is automatically high-risk. Article 6(3) lets a system escape the high-risk tier if it does not pose a significant risk because it only:

  • performs a narrow procedural task; or
  • improves the result of a completed human activity; or
  • detects decision patterns/deviations without replacing human review; or
  • performs a preparatory task.

Example: a tool that only formats and forwards applications to a recruiter, without scoring or ranking, may qualify for the downgrade. But the moment it ranks, scores, or filters candidates, it is doing the consequential work - and it is high-risk.

LiveThe profiling trap3 min▶
  • The override: if the system performs profiling of natural persons, the Article 6(3) downgrade does not apply - it is high-risk, full stop.
  • Why it matters: teams reach for the downgrade to avoid the compliance burden, then forget that any real evaluation of a person (scoring, ranking, predicting behaviour) is profiling. The escape hatch closes.
  • The honest test: "does this system make or shape a judgement about a specific person?" If yes, assume high-risk and prove otherwise, not the reverse.
Real world

Vantage classifies its hiring tool. Vantage hoped its CV screener might qualify for the Article 6(3) downgrade as a "preparatory" tool. But it ranks candidates - that is profiling. No downgrade: it is high-risk under Annex III(4). The classification decided Vantage's entire compliance roadmap for the rest of the track.

Check yourself

Three questions before you go 🎯 ◐ 90 seconds

1 · You classify a system in order. What do you check FIRST (after confirming it is in scope)?

First match wins, and prohibited is checked first: if it matches Article 5 it cannot ship, and no lower tier is relevant. Then high-risk, then limited, else minimal.

2 · An Annex III system might avoid the high-risk tier via the Article 6(3) downgrade - UNLESS it...

The downgrade covers narrow/preparatory systems, but profiling of people overrides it: any system that scores, ranks, or predicts about a person is high-risk, no downgrade.

3 · An AI CV-screening tool that ranks candidates, used on EU applicants, is...

Employment/recruitment is Annex III(4). Ranking candidates is profiling, so the Article 6(3) downgrade does not apply - it is high-risk, with the full obligations.
