Frameworks tell you what good looks like. ISO/IEC 42001 lets you prove it. Part 0

From framework to proof

You have met the EU AI Act (the law) and NIST (the risk method). Both tell you what to do. Neither hands you a certificate. ISO/IEC 42001 is different: it is a management-system standard, built like ISO 27001, that an accredited body audits and certifies. That certificate is the thing a nervous customer or a regulator can actually check. This session is warm and practical - the goal is that by the end you can sketch your own AI management system on one page.

Part 1 · the management system

The AI management system (AIMS) 9 min live

ISO/IEC 42001:2023 is the world's first certifiable AI management-system standard. If you have seen ISO 27001 for information security, the shape is familiar: a policy, roles, risk work, a set of reference controls, and a loop that never stops turning.

LiveWhat an AIMS actually contains3 min▶

An AI management system is not a document - it is a running system with named parts. ISO/IEC 42001 asks you to put these in place:

  • An AI policy and objectives - a signed statement of how the organisation intends to develop and use AI, and what it is trying to achieve.
  • Roles and responsibilities - who owns AI governance, who owns each system, who reviews and signs off.
  • An AI risk assessment - the standard risk lens: what could go wrong, how likely, how bad.
  • An AI system impact assessment - a distinct step that looks at effects on individuals, groups, and society, not just on the business.
  • Controls from Annex A - a reference set of controls you select from and justify.
  • Continual improvement - the Check and Act half of the loop: audits, monitoring, management review, corrective action.

LiveRisk assessment vs impact assessment3 min▶
  • AI risk assessment asks "what could hurt us, and how do we treat it?" - the familiar likelihood-times-consequence view aimed at the organisation and its objectives.
  • AI system impact assessment asks "who does this system affect, and how?" - the effects on individuals, groups, and society. This is the step teams from a pure security background tend to skip.
  • Why both: a system can be low-risk to the business and high-impact on the people it touches. A hiring tool is a clean example - reliable for the company, life-shaping for the candidate.
Self-studyThe ISO 27001 parallel2 min▶

ISO/IEC 42001 borrows ISO 27001's Harmonized Structure on purpose. If your organisation already holds ISO 27001, the plumbing is shared: the same clause layout, the same "Statement of Applicability" idea for controls, the same audit rhythm, often the same internal team. You are not learning a new discipline - you are pointing an existing one at AI. That reuse is one of the strongest practical arguments for choosing 42001 over standing up a bespoke governance programme from scratch.

Part 2 · why it matters

Certification, and why it matters 9 min live

A framework you follow privately is a promise. A certificate is evidence. The single most useful thing about ISO/IEC 42001 is that an accredited body can audit you and issue a certificate a third party trusts - the exact artefact a customer or regulator asks for.

LiveAuditable and certifiable - the differentiator3 min▶
  • Independently auditable. Certification runs as a two-stage audit by an accredited body - stage 1 reviews your documentation, stage 2 tests whether the system actually runs the way the documents say.
  • Certifiable. Pass, and you hold a certificate a third party trusts. This is the practical line between ISO/IEC 42001 and a voluntary framework: NIST gives you a method, 42001 gives you proof.
  • Evidence, not assertion. When a customer or regulator asks "show me your AI is governed", a certificate answers in a way a slide deck cannot.
LiveThe Annex A reference controls3 min▶

Annex A gives you a reference set of controls to select from and justify - grouped under a handful of control objectives spanning policy, resources, the AI lifecycle, data, information for interested parties, and use of AI systems.

Verify the count Annex A is commonly reported as roughly 38 controls across 9 control objectives, but exact numbers vary between summaries. Treat it as "a reference control set" and confirm the precise figure against the published standard - do not quote a number you have not checked in the text itself.

You do not implement every control blindly. As in ISO 27001, you decide which apply to your context, justify inclusions and exclusions, and record that reasoning - the "why we chose these" is part of the evidence.

Self-studyWhere 42001 sits next to the law2 min▶

ISO/IEC 42001 is not the EU AI Act and does not replace it. The Act is mandatory law; 42001 is a voluntary management-system standard. But a strong AIMS is a natural way to produce and organise the evidence the Act expects - risk work, documentation, roles, monitoring. The pattern to remember for b8: build to the legal floor, and use a standard like 42001 to make your compliance auditable and repeatable rather than heroic and one-off.

Vantage answers the evidence request. Vantage's German enterprise customer stopped accepting assurances and asked for independent proof that Vantage's high-risk hiring tool is properly governed. Build-along · take it further

Sketch Vantage's AIMS ★ 10 min · one page

You do not need the full standard to draft the skeleton. Sketch an AI management system for Vantage on a single page.

AI policy. Write one sentence of Vantage's AI policy - how it intends to build and use AI, and what it is trying to achieve (fair, lawful, auditable hiring support).

An owner. Name the role that owns the AIMS. Who signs the policy, and who reviews it each cycle?

One risk + one impact. Write one line of AI risk (to Vantage) and one line of AI system impact (on candidates). Notice how different they are.

Three controls. Pick three reference controls you would apply first - for example, a control on data quality, one on human oversight, and one on information provided to affected people. Justify each in a few words.

Check yourself

Three questions before you go 🎯 ◐ 90 seconds

1 · What makes ISO/IEC 42001:2023 stand out from voluntary frameworks like NIST?

ISO/IEC 42001 is the first certifiable AI management-system standard: an accredited body audits you and issues a certificate. That auditable evidence is the practical differentiator from a framework you merely follow.

2 · Besides an AI risk assessment, ISO/IEC 42001 asks you to run a distinct...

Risk assessment looks at threats to the organisation; the impact assessment looks outward at effects on the people and society the system touches. A management system needs both lenses.

3 · Annex A of ISO/IEC 42001 is best described as...

Like ISO 27001, Annex A is a reference control set. You choose which controls apply to your context and record the reasoning - inclusions and exclusions are part of the evidence.
