# Presenter notes - Leader session 5: NIST AI RMF, ISO 42001 & the SG framework

**Session goal (one line).** Leaders can explain why the law alone is not a program and pick which framework - NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001, or Singapore's Model AI Governance Framework - to start with.

## Run of show (45 min)
- **0-3 - Welcome.** The law tells you what is mandatory; it does not tell you how to run governance. That is what the frameworks are for.
- **3-20 - Concepts (Part 1 + Part 2).**
  - Part 1 (approx 9 min): the framework map - law, method, proof. Reinforce the one line from a1.
  - Part 2 (approx 8 min): NIST AI 100-1 (Govern, Map, Measure, Manage) and ISO/IEC 42001 (a PDCA management system) in one look; where the SG Model Framework fits for APAC.
- **20-40 - Exercises.**
  - Exercise 1 "Map need to framework" (approx 12 min): match a governance need to the right framework.
  - Exercise 2 "Pick your starting framework" (approx 8 min): choose one to begin with and justify it.
- **40-45 - Q&A.** Field "do we need all three" questions; bridge to program-building in a6.

## Preflight
- Open a5, "Expand all", confirm the framework-map SVG and the NIST/ISO comparison render.
- Be ready to say plainly: NIST = method, ISO 42001 = certifiable proof, EU AI Act = binding law, SG framework = voluntary APAC reference.
- Do not quote a hard ISO Annex A control count from memory - verify against the standard if asked.

## Never-cut beats
- **The three do different jobs.** Method (NIST), proof (ISO 42001), law (the Act). You do not pick one and drop the others - a mature program uses all three.
- **Certifiable is the ISO differentiator.** Only ISO/IEC 42001 gives you a third party that can audit and certify you - proof you can hand a customer, not a promise.
- **Start where the pressure is.** If a customer wants evidence, ISO. If teams assess risk ad hoc, NIST first.

## Cuts if running long
- Treat the SG Model Framework as a one-line "APAC voluntary reference" rather than a full walk.
- Merge Exercise 1 and 2 into a single "pick and justify" pass.

## Quiz answers
1. **B** - NIST is a voluntary method, ISO 42001 is certifiable proof, the Act is binding law; three jobs, not interchangeable.
2. **C** - ISO/IEC 42001 is the first certifiable AI management-system standard - a third party can audit and certify you.
3. **A** - The four NIST AI RMF functions are Govern, Map, Measure, Manage (not PDCA, not the cybersecurity framework).

## Common questions + crisp answers
- "Do we have to be certified to ISO 42001?" No - it is voluntary. But certification is the strongest way to evidence governance.
- "Is NIST enough on its own?" As a method, yes for reasoning about risk; but it is not law and it does not certify you.
- "How many controls are in ISO 42001 Annex A?" It is a reference control set you select from and justify; confirm the exact count against the current standard.

## Teaching tips
- Reuse the a1 one line as the spine of the whole session; by now it should be a chant, not a new idea.
- Leaders want a single answer to "which one?" Resist it - the honest answer is "all three, in different roles" - but give them a defensible starting pick based on their pressure.
- Certifiable is the word that earns ISO its place. If a customer or regulator wants proof, only ISO 42001 hands them a certificate.
- Keep NIST as "the method your teams already need" rather than a compliance chore; it is the common language, not another audit.
- Do not bluff the ISO Annex A control count. "It is a reference set you select from and justify" is both the correct and the more useful answer.
