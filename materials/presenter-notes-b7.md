# Presenter notes - Practitioner session 7: Risk assessment + conformity

**Session goal (one line).** Practitioners can distinguish the deployer's fundamental rights impact assessment (Article 27) from the provider's conformity route, and map a system's pre-market path.

## Run of show (45 min)
- **0-3 - Welcome.** Two machines, one market gate: the FRIA (deployer) and conformity assessment (provider) both feed whether a high-risk system may go to market.
- **3-22 - The FRIA (Part 1).** The fundamental rights impact assessment - a deployer duty, done before first use, for public bodies and deployers of certain Annex III systems (credit scoring, insurance). Distinct from the provider's Article 9 risk-management system.
- **22-40 - Conformity (Part 2 + build-along).** The provider's route - self-assessment (Annex VI) default vs notified-body audit (Annex VII), then declaration, CE marking, and EU database registration; then the build-along "Map Vantage's pre-market path."
- **40-45 - Q&A.** Bridge to the operating model in b8.

## Preflight
- Open b7, "Expand all", confirm the FRIA and conformity-route SVGs render.
- Be clear on the Annex VI (self-assessment) default vs the Annex VII notified-body exception for point-1 biometrics.
- Have the correct pre-market sequence memorised: declaration, CE marking, registration.

## Never-cut beats
- **FRIA is the deployer's, not the provider's.** Done before first use, for public bodies and deployers of certain Annex III systems. Do not confuse it with the provider's Article 9 risk management.
- **Self-assessment is the default.** For Annex III points 2 to 8, conformity is internal control under Annex VI; a notified body (Annex VII) is required for point-1 biometrics where harmonised standards are absent.
- **The pre-market order.** Declaration of conformity (Art 47), then CE marking (Art 48), then EU database registration (Art 49) - all before placing on the market.

## Cuts if running long
- Map only Vantage's conformity route in the build-along, leaving the FRIA as a worked example on the slide.
- Summarise the Annex VII exception verbally.

## Quiz answers
1. **C** - Deployer: the FRIA (Art 27) is a deployer duty by public bodies and deployers of certain Annex III systems, before first use.
2. **A** - Self-assessment / internal control under Annex VI is the default for Annex III points 2 to 8.
3. **B** - Order: EU declaration of conformity, then CE marking, then EU database registration.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- "We built the system - do we do the FRIA?" Not as provider; the FRIA is the deployer's duty (though you support it). Your route is conformity assessment.
- "Do we always need a notified body?" No - self-assessment is the default; a notified body applies mainly to point-1 biometrics without harmonised standards.
- "CE marking before or after the declaration?" After: declaration, then CE marking, then registration.

## Teaching tips
- Keep the two machines on separate sides of the board: FRIA (deployer, before use) and conformity (provider, before market). Learners blur them constantly.
- Correct the "notified body always" assumption immediately - self-assessment is the default, and that changes the cost picture a lot.
- Drill the pre-market order as a fixed sequence: declaration, CE marking, registration. Out-of-order answers are the common quiz miss.
- Map Vantage's path end to end so the abstract route becomes a checklist they can copy for their own system.
- If the room is deployer-heavy, spend more on the FRIA; if provider-heavy, more on the conformity route - tailor to who is in the seats.
