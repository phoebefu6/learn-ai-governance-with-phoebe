# Presenter notes - Leader session 3: Roles + timeline

**Session goal (one line).** Leaders can name their role in the AI value chain (provider, deployer, importer, distributor) for each system and place their obligations on the Act's application timeline.

## Run of show (45 min)
- **0-3 - Welcome.** Two things decide your workload: your role and your date. Get both right and the obligations become concrete.
- **3-20 - Concepts (Part 1 + Part 2).**
  - Part 1 (approx 9 min): the roles in the chain. Provider carries the most; deployer has its own duties; importer and distributor sit between. Role follows what you do to the system, not your industry.
  - Part 2 (approx 8 min): the application dates. Entry into force Aug 2024, prohibitions Feb 2025, GPAI Aug 2025, most high-risk (Annex III) Aug 2026.
- **20-40 - Exercises.**
  - Exercise 1 "Name your role for each system" (approx 12 min): learners tag provider vs deployer per system.
  - Exercise 2 "Place your systems on the timeline" (approx 8 min): map each obligation to its date.
- **40-45 - Q&A.** Field role-change questions; preview penalties in a4.

## Preflight
- Open a3, "Expand all", confirm the roles diagram and the timeline SVG render on the projector.
- Have each learner's a1 scope list handy - this session tags roles onto it.
- Be ready to explain the "deployer becomes provider" trap with a concrete modification example.

## Never-cut beats
- **Role follows what you do, not who you are.** Substantially modify a high-risk system, or put your own name on it, and you can become the provider and inherit the full Article 16 programme.
- **The date that matters is Aug 2026.** Most Annex III high-risk obligations and Article 50 transparency apply from 2 Aug 2026 - plan to the earlier date even though an amendment may push it.
- **A non-EU provider likely needs an EU authorised representative.** Do not let that surprise anyone late.

## Cuts if running long
- Compress importer/distributor to one line; most learners are provider or deployer.
- Turn Exercise 2 into a quick show-of-hands on the Aug 2026 date rather than a full plot.

## Quiz answers
1. **A** - Provider: it develops the system and places it on the market under its own name (Art 3, 16), the heaviest role.
2. **C** - 2 Aug 2026: when most of the Act, including Annex III high-risk and Article 50, applies.
3. **B** - It can become the provider and inherit the heavy obligations by substantially modifying or rebranding the system.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- "We just buy and use a vendor tool - are we off the hook?" You are a deployer, with your own duties (oversight, logging, informing workers); not off the hook.
- "Has Aug 2026 moved?" A proposed delay exists but is not final. Plan to Aug 2026 and adjust if it lands.
- "Do we need an EU representative?" Non-EU providers of in-scope systems generally do - check early.

## Teaching tips
- Two variables, one sentence: "your role decides what you owe, your date decides when." Write it on the board and keep returning to it.
- The deployer-becomes-provider trap catches procurement-heavy firms. Make it concrete with a "we bought it and tweaked it" story.
- Do not let the timeline become a reason to wait. The point of the dates is to plan backwards from Aug 2026, not to relax until then.
- Have learners tag roles directly onto their a1 scope list - reusing prior work keeps the session grounded and fast.
- If asked about the EU authorised representative, treat it as a practical to-do, not a footnote; non-EU providers often forget it until late.

## Bridge to next session
a4 attaches a number to all of this - the fine tiers and who enforces them - which is what makes the board care about getting roles and dates right.

## Compliance caution
Educational, not legal advice; the high-risk application timeline is under live amendment (the proposed Digital Omnibus could shift Annex III dates to around Dec 2027) - plan to the earlier date and verify against the Regulation.
