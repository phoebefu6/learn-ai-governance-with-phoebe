# Presenter notes - Leader session 1: Why AI governance now + the Act's reach

**Session goal (one line).** Leaders walk out able to say why AI governance is now a legal obligation, name the three-layer framework map, and run the one test for whether the EU AI Act reaches their Singapore company.

## Run of show (45 min)
- **0-3 - Welcome.** Set the frame: governance used to be a values statement, now it is binding law with fines up to 7% of global turnover. Name the Vantage running case (the German customer asking for conformity docs).
- **3-20 - Concepts (Part 1 + Part 2).**
  - Part 1 (approx 9 min): law, method, proof. Land the one line - "NIST shows what good looks like, ISO 42001 lets you prove it, the Act makes parts mandatory." Walk the three-layer SVG.
  - Part 2 (approx 8 min): Article 2 reach. Draw the market-and-output model on the whiteboard, spend your time on the output-in-the-Union limb.
- **20-40 - Exercises.**
  - Exercise 1 "Does the Act reach you?" (12 min, pen and paper): learners list their AI systems, mark in/out of scope, note provider vs deployer, circle the highest-stakes in-scope one.
  - Exercise 2 "Map your frameworks" (8 min): the four diagnostic questions that reveal program vs slide.
- **40-45 - Q&A.** Field scope questions; park deep tier questions for a2.

## Preflight
- Open a1 on the projector; hit "Expand all" once so nothing is hidden mid-session.
- Toggle "Projector zoom" on and confirm the two SVGs (three layers, Article 2 flow) are legible from the back.
- Have a whiteboard ready for the Article 2 trigger.
- Skim the Vantage case so you can tell the German-customer story from memory.
- Have the five team questions (end of page) ready to send to their AI, legal, and data teams.

## Never-cut beats
- **The one line.** "NIST shows what good looks like, ISO 42001 lets you prove it, the EU AI Act makes parts mandatory." This is the mental model the whole course hangs on.
- **The output-in-the-Union catch.** A model run entirely in Singapore, whose results decide things about EU people, is in scope. This is the moment leaders realise it is their problem.
- **The board number.** EUR 35M or 7% of global turnover - bigger than the GDPR's top tier.

## Cuts if running long
- Drop the "Governance is bigger than the Act" self-study card (it reads fine after class).
- Shorten Exercise 2 to just the first and third questions.
- Skip zooming both SVGs; the three-layer one carries the concept.

## Quiz answers
1. **C** - In scope: the output is used in the Union (Article 2 follows the effect, not the office).
2. **A** - The Act is binding law, NIST is a voluntary method, ISO 42001 is a certifiable system; three layers, not competitors.
3. **B** - Up to EUR 35M or 7% of global annual turnover for prohibited-practice breaches (Art 99).

## Common questions + crisp answers
- "We have no EU office - are we safe?" No. Reach follows where the output lands, not where you sit.
- "What if only a few EU users are affected?" Still in scope; there is no de minimis carve-out on the output limb. Check, do not assume.
- "Provider or deployer - does it really change much?" Yes, it decides who carries the heavy obligations. We settle this in a3.

## Teaching tips
- This is the anchor session; if learners leave with only the one line and the Article 2 test, you have won. Everything else is reinforcement.
- Keep it non-technical. This is a leader room - talk in decisions, liability, and evidence, not in article numbers.
- Use the German-customer moment as the emotional hook: it turns "someday" into "this quarter" better than any statute.
- When someone says "that seems like overreach," agree it feels that way, then point at the GDPR precedent they already live with - it lands as familiar, not alien.
- End by handing out the five team questions; a session that produces homework someone actually sends is a session that sticks.

## Bridge to next session

## Compliance caution
Educational, not legal advice; the EU AI Act is new and its high-risk timeline is under live amendment (the proposed Digital Omnibus) - verify against the Regulation text and get proper advice before acting.
