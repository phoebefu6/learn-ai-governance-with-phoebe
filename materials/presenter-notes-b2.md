# Presenter notes - Practitioner session 2: High-risk obligations

**Session goal (one line).** Practitioners can list the seven system requirements a high-risk system must meet (Articles 9-15), the provider wrapper around them, and the deployer's separate duties (Article 26).

## Run of show (45 min)
- **0-3 - Welcome.** What "high-risk" actually costs you: seven requirements plus a provider wrapper plus deployer duties - none optional.
- **3-18 - Concepts (Part 1 + Part 2).**
  - Part 1 (approx 8 min): the seven the system must meet (Art 9-15) - risk management, data governance, documentation, logging, transparency, human oversight, accuracy/robustness/security.
  - Part 2 (approx 7 min): the provider wrapper (Annex IV docs, CE marking, EU database registration) and the deployer's part (Art 26).
- **18-40 - Build-along "Build the high-risk obligations checklist" (approx 22 min).** Learners assemble a working checklist for a high-risk system, splitting provider vs deployer rows.
- **40-45 - Q&A.** Preview transparency in b3.

## Preflight
- Open b2, "Expand all", confirm the seven-requirements SVG and the provider/deployer split render.
- Have a blank checklist template ready (or the on-page structure) so the build-along produces something reusable.
- Be crisp on which duties are provider vs deployer before you present.

## Never-cut beats
- **Seven, and all seven must hold.** There is no "six of seven" - a high-risk system is compliant only when every Article 9-15 requirement is met.
- **Provider vs deployer is a real split.** Annex IV documentation, CE marking, and provider registration sit with the provider; use-per-instructions, oversight, logs (6+ months), and informing workers sit with the deployer (Art 26).
- **Data governance is an obligation, not a nice-to-have.** Article 10 requires relevant, representative, as-error-free-as-possible data with harmful bias identified and mitigated.

## Cuts if running long
- Build the checklist for the seven system requirements only; leave the wrapper as a listed reference.
- Summarise the deployer duties verbally instead of adding every row.

## Quiz answers
1. **C** - Seven requirements, and all must hold; there is no "six of seven."
2. **A** - Deployer duty: keep logs at least 6 months and inform workers before workplace use (Art 26); docs/CE/registration are the provider's.
3. **B** - Training data must be relevant, representative, and as error-free as possible, with harmful bias identified and mitigated (Art 10).

## Common questions + crisp answers
- "We only use the vendor's system - which of these are ours?" The Article 26 deployer set: oversight, logging, informing workers, monitoring and reporting.
- "Can we defer logging?" No - logging is one of the seven and it is not discretionary.
- "Is CE marking ours?" Only if you are the provider; deployers do not affix CE marking.

## Teaching tips
- Make the checklist an artifact they keep. The value of this session is a reusable provider/deployer split, not a lecture on seven articles.
- Colour-code provider rows vs deployer rows as you build; the split is the thing practitioners get wrong in the field.
- Land "all seven must hold" as a hard gate. Teams love to treat logging or documentation as optional polish - it is not.
- Article 10 data governance is where engineers lean in; use it to connect this back to their existing data quality work.
- If a learner is a deployer only, keep them focused on the Article 26 rows so they leave with their actual obligations, not the provider's.
