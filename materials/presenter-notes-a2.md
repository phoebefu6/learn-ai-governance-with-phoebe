# Presenter notes - Leader session 2: The risk tiers

**Session goal (one line).** Leaders can place any AI system in the right EU AI Act tier - prohibited, high-risk, limited, minimal - and know the two lists (Article 5 bans, Annex III high-risk) that decide the outcome.

## Run of show (45 min)
- **0-3 - Welcome.** Frame it: the tier is the whole game. Everything downstream - obligations, cost, timeline - flows from which tier a system lands in.
- **3-20 - Concepts (Part 1 + Part 2).**
  - Part 1 (approx 9 min): the pyramid. Walk the four tiers top to bottom and stress "first match wins" - you check for a ban before anything else.
  - Part 2 (approx 8 min): the two decisive lists - Article 5 prohibited practices and Annex III high-risk areas. Employment/recruitment is Annex III area 4.
- **20-40 - Exercises.**
  - Exercise 1 "Sort six systems into tiers" (approx 12 min): learners tier six sample systems, defending each call.
  - Exercise 2 "The 'any prohibited?' scan" (approx 8 min): run every system past Article 5 first.
- **40-45 - Q&A.** Take tier edge-cases; note that b1 makes this hands-on with the classifier.

## Preflight

## Never-cut beats
- **The Article 6(3) downgrade AND its limit.** An Annex III system can sometimes drop out of high-risk if it is narrow or preparatory - BUT if it profiles natural persons the downgrade never applies. Say the sentence out loud: "profiling is always high-risk."
- **Prohibited means stop, not fix.** Emotion recognition in the workplace (Art 5) has no compliance path. There is no checklist that makes a banned use legal.
- **First match wins.** Check prohibited first, then high-risk, then limited, else minimal.

## Cuts if running long
- Trim Exercise 1 from six systems to four.
- Fold the limited/minimal distinction into one sentence and spend the time on prohibited vs high-risk.

## Quiz answers
1. **B** - High-risk: candidate screening is employment/recruitment under Annex III area 4 (and profiling blocks the downgrade).
2. **C** - Prohibited: emotion recognition in the workplace is an Article 5 banned practice, no compliance path.
3. **A** - A GPAI model is presumed systemic-risk when training compute exceeds 10^25 FLOP (Art 51).

## Common questions + crisp answers
- "Can we argue our hiring tool is low-stakes and downgrade it?" No - it profiles people, so Article 6(3) is off the table.
- "Is all biometric AI banned?" No. Some biometric uses are prohibited, others are high-risk; the specific use decides.
- "Who decides the tier - us or the regulator?" You classify and must be able to defend it; the regulator can disagree, so document your reasoning.

## Teaching tips
- The whole session rests on one habit: check for a ban first, then work down the pyramid. Drill "first match wins" until it is automatic.
- The profiling override is the single most missed point in the Act. Say it three times in three ways so nobody leaves thinking they can downgrade a scoring tool.
- Keep prohibited vs high-risk sharp: prohibited means stop, high-risk means comply. Leaders conflate them and then under-react to bans.
- Use the six-system sort as a debate, not a quiz - the disagreements are where the learning is. Let them argue the edge cases.
- If a leader insists their system is "just software," ask what decision it shapes and about whom; that question re-tiers most systems on the spot.
