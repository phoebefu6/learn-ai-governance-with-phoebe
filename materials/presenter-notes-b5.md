# Presenter notes - Practitioner session 5: NIST AI RMF hands-on

**Session goal (one line).** Practitioners can run a mini Govern-Map-Measure-Manage pass and name the seven trustworthy-AI characteristics the framework is built to serve.

## Run of show (45 min)
- **0-3 - Welcome.** Why reach for a voluntary framework: it gives teams a common language for AI risk that maps onto the Act and ISO.
- **3-22 - Govern-Map-Measure-Manage (Part 1).** The four functions, with Govern as the central, continuous one running through the other three.
- **22-40 - The 7 traits + a pass (Part 2 + build-along).** The seven trustworthy-AI characteristics, then the build-along "Run a mini GMMM pass" on a real system.
- **40-45 - Q&A.** Bridge to ISO 42001 proof in b6.

## Preflight
- Open b5, "Expand all", confirm the GMMM diagram and the seven-traits SVG render.
- Pick a real system (or Vantage's hiring tool) for the GMMM pass.
- Have the seven traits memorised so you can catch the "which is NOT one" quiz cleanly.

## Never-cut beats
- **Govern is central and continuous.** It cultivates the risk culture - oversight, accountability, policies, roles - while Map, Measure, and Manage cycle around it.
- **Voluntary, US-origin, no certificate.** The RMF (NIST AI 100-1, Jan 2023) is a method, not a law and not a pass/fail; it complements the binding Act.
- **The seven traits are the target.** Valid and reliable; safe; secure and resilient; accountable and transparent; explainable and interpretable; privacy-enhanced; fair with harmful bias managed. "Profitable and scalable" is not among them.

## Cuts if running long
- Run the GMMM pass through Govern and Map only, and describe Measure/Manage.
- List the seven traits without discussing each; the exercise carries them.

## Quiz answers
1. **B** - Govern is the central, continuous function running through Map, Measure, and Manage.
2. **C** - A voluntary, US-origin framework with no certification - method, not law.
3. **A** - "Profitable and scalable" is NOT one of the seven trustworthy-AI characteristics.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- "Do we get certified in NIST?" No - it is a voluntary method with no certificate; certification comes from ISO 42001 (b6).
- "Where do we start a GMMM pass?" With Govern - set the roles and accountability, then Map the context and risks.
- "How does this relate to the Act?" It is the method for reasoning about risk that the Act's obligations assume you already have.

## Teaching tips
- Run the GMMM pass on a real system so it produces notes, not theory. The output is a starter risk file they can keep.
- Put Govern at the centre visually and keep returning to it; learners default to treating it as just another step.
- Frame the seven traits as acceptance criteria, not vocabulary. "Would we sign off that this system is fair and explainable?" is the useful test.
- Keep reminding the room this is voluntary and complementary - it is the method the Act's obligations assume you already run.
- If time is tight, a strong Govern-and-Map pass beats a rushed all-four sprint; depth on two functions teaches the loop better.

## Bridge to next session
b6 turns method into proof: ISO/IEC 42001, the management system that lets you evidence the governance you just practised.

## Compliance caution
Educational, not legal advice; the NIST AI RMF is a voluntary framework that does not by itself satisfy the EU AI Act - verify obligations against the Regulation and get advice.
