# Presenter notes - Practitioner session 6: ISO/IEC 42001

**Session goal (one line).** Practitioners can describe the AI management system (AIMS), the certification path, and sketch what an AIMS looks like for the running case.

## Run of show (45 min)
- **0-3 - Welcome.** From framework to proof: ISO/IEC 42001 is how you run governance operationally and evidence it to a third party.
- **3-22 - The AIMS (Part 1).** The management system - PDCA structure, policy, risk assessment, the AI system impact assessment, and Annex A as a reference control set you select from and justify.
- **22-40 - Certification (Part 2 + build-along).** Why certification matters (auditable third-party proof), then the build-along "Sketch Vantage's AIMS."
- **40-45 - Q&A.** Bridge to risk assessment and conformity in b7.

## Preflight
- Open b6, "Expand all", confirm the AIMS diagram renders.
- Do NOT quote a hard Annex A control count from memory - verify against the published standard if a learner asks.
- Have the Vantage case handy so the AIMS sketch is concrete.

## Never-cut beats
- **Certifiable is the whole point.** ISO/IEC 42001:2023 is the first certifiable AI management-system standard - an accredited body audits you and issues a certificate. Auditable evidence, not a promise.
- **Two lenses, not one.** Besides the AI risk assessment (threats to the organisation), the standard asks for a distinct AI system impact assessment (effects on individuals, groups, and society).
- **Annex A is a reference set.** Like ISO 27001, you choose which controls apply and record the reasoning; inclusions and exclusions are part of the evidence.

## Cuts if running long
- Sketch only the policy, risk assessment, and impact assessment layers of the AIMS.
- Describe the certification steps verbally rather than walking the full cycle.

## Quiz answers
1. **B** - It is independently auditable and certifiable - it yields third-party proof, unlike a framework you merely follow.
2. **C** - An AI system impact assessment - effects on individuals, groups, and society - distinct from the risk assessment.
3. **A** - Annex A is a reference set of controls you select from and justify, not a fixed must-implement-all list.

## Common questions + crisp answers
- "Does ISO 42001 replace the EU AI Act?" No - it is voluntary; it helps you evidence compliance, it does not remove the legal obligations.
- "Do we implement every Annex A control?" No - you select the applicable ones and document why the rest are excluded.
- "Risk assessment vs impact assessment - same thing?" No - one looks inward at threats to you, the other outward at effects on people.

## Teaching tips
- Lead with the certificate. Practitioners engage once they see this is the artifact a customer or auditor accepts, not another internal doc.
- Keep the two assessments distinct on the board - risk assessment (inward) vs impact assessment (outward) - because teams merge them and lose the outward lens.
- Sketch the AIMS as a living system with a PDCA loop, not a binder; the management-system idea is what separates ISO from a checklist.
- On Annex A, teach the select-and-justify discipline explicitly - the justification for exclusions is itself the evidence.
- Do not invent a control count. If pressed, say verify against the standard; correctness here protects the learner's own credibility later.
